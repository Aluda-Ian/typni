FAQ

1. What is TYPNI, and what is its mission?

TYPNI (The Young People's Network International) is a youth-focused organization in Kenya spearheaded by Charlene Ruto, the daughter of Kenyaâ€™s President. Our mission is to empower young people by providing them with the tools, resources, and opportunities they need to thrive. Through initiatives like youth mentorship, entrepreneurship, education, and sports, we aim to create a generation of confident, skilled, and visionary leaders who can drive positive change in their communities and beyond.

2. How can I donate to TYPNI, and where will my donation go?

Donating to TYPNI is easy! You can make a one-time or recurring donation through our secure online platform. Your contributions support various programs, including:
Funding for youth entrepreneurship and startups. Scholarships and educational resources for underprivileged youth.
Sports and talent development programs.
Community outreach and empowerment projects.
We ensure transparency and accountability in how donations are used, and you can stay updated on the impact of your contribution through our reports and success stories. Visit our Donate page to contribute today.

3. How can I get involved with TYPNI?

There are several ways to get involved with TYPNI:
Volunteer: Join our team of passionate volunteers and contribute your time and skills to our programs.
Donate: Support our initiatives by making a financial contribution to fund youth empowerment projects.
Partner: Collaborate with us as a corporate or organizational partner to create more opportunities for young people.
Participate: Join our workshops, mentorship programs, or events to learn, grow, and connect with others.

4. What initiatives does TYPNI focus on?

TYPNI focuses on a wide range of initiatives designed to empower young people, including:
Youth Empowerment and Mentorship
Entrepreneurship and Financial Literacy
Education and Skills Development
Sports, Arts, and Talent Development
Climate Change Mitigation and Adaptation
Mental Health and Inclusivity
Advocacy for Youth Representation
